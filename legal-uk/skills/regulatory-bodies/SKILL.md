## 2. Complaint Routes by Regulator

### ICO

1. Raise complaint with the organisation first (data controller has 1 calendar month to respond under UK GDPR Art 12)
2. If unresolved, complain to ICO via ico.org.uk (online form)
3. ICO may investigate, issue enforcement notice, or impose monetary penalty
4. Appeal: First-Tier Tribunal (General Regulatory Chamber)

### FCA

1. Complain to the firm (8 weeks to issue final response)
2. If unresolved, refer to **Financial Ombudsman Service** (FOS) within 6 months of final response
3. FOS award binding on firm up to £430,000 (from April 2024)
4. FCA enforcement: own-initiative investigations; Regulatory Decisions Committee
5. Appeal: Upper Tribunal (Tax and Chancery Chamber)

### CMA

1. Report anti-competitive behaviour at gov.uk/cma
2. CMA may open investigation under Chapter I/II of Competition Act 1998
3. Leniency available for first whistle-blower in cartel cases
4. Appeal: Competition Appeal Tribunal (CAT)

### HSE

1. Report workplace safety concerns to HSE at hse.gov.uk
2. HSE may inspect premises, issue improvement/prohibition notices
3. Prosecution in magistrates' court or Crown Court
4. Appeal against notices: Employment Tribunal (within 21 days)

### SRA

1. Complain to the solicitor's firm first
2. Service complaints: Legal Ombudsman (within 1 year of act/6 months of final response)
3. Conduct complaints: SRA directly at sra.org.uk
4. SRA may investigate and refer to Solicitors Disciplinary Tribunal (SDT)
5. Appeal from SDT: High Court (Administrative Court)

## 3. Auto-Enrolment (Workplace Pensions)

**Primary legislation:** Pensions Act 2008, Employers' Duties (Implementation) Regulations 2010 (SI 2010/4), as amended.

**Regulator:** The Pensions Regulator (TPR).

### Employer Duties

Every employer with **one or more workers** in the UK must comply with auto-enrolment duties. There are no exemptions based on employer size.

### Worker Categories

| Category | Age | Earnings | Employer Must... |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Eligible jobholder** | 22 to State Pension age | >£10,000/yr (£833/month, £192/week) | **Auto-enrol** into qualifying pension scheme |
| **Non-eligible jobholder** | 16–21 or SPA–74 earning >£10,000; OR 22–SPA earning £6,240–£10,000 | See age/earnings combination | Provide right to **opt in** on request |
| **Entitled worker** | 16–74 | ≤£6,240/yr | Provide right to **join** a pension scheme (no employer contribution required) |

### Qualifying Earnings Band 2025/26

- **Lower limit:** £6,240 per year
- **Upper limit:** £50,270 per year
- Contributions calculated on earnings between these limits (the "qualifying earnings" band)

### Minimum Contribution Rates

| Contributor | Minimum % of Qualifying Earnings |
|---|---|
| **Employer** | **3%** (minimum) |
| **Employee** | **5%** (including tax relief) |
| **Total** | **8%** |

The employer must contribute at least 3%. The remaining 5% comes from the employee
(4% from pay + 1% from tax relief under net pay or relief at source arrangement).

### Auto-Enrolment Calculator Workflow

```
Step 1: Identify the worker
        → Age: ___    Gross annual earnings: £___

Step 2: Classify the worker
        → IF age 22–SPA AND earnings > £10,000 → ELIGIBLE JOBHOLDER → Go to Step 3
        → IF non-eligible jobholder → inform of opt-in right → STOP (unless they opt in)
        → IF entitled worker → inform of right to join → STOP

Step 3: Check existing pension membership
        → IF already in qualifying scheme with min contributions → DUTY MET
        → IF not → proceed to auto-enrol

Step 4: Auto-enrol into qualifying scheme
        → Enrolment date: typically first day of employment or day they become eligible
        → Provide statutory notice (letter) within 6 weeks

Step 5: Calculate contributions on qualifying earnings
        → Qualifying earnings = Gross pay − £6,240 (annual) [capped at £50,270]
        → Employer contribution: qualifying earnings × 3% (minimum)
        → Employee contribution: qualifying earnings × 5% (minimum, incl. tax relief)

        EXAMPLE: Worker earns £30,000/yr
          Qualifying earnings: £30,000 − £6,240 = £23,760
          Employer min (3%):  £23,760 × 3% = £712.80/yr (£59.40/month)
          Employee min (5%):  £23,760 × 5% = £1,188.00/yr (£99.00/month)
          Total (8%):         £23,760 × 8% = £1,900.80/yr (£158.40/month)

Step 6: Handle opt-outs
        → Worker has 1 month opt-out window from enrolment date
        → If opt-out: refund all employee contributions; employer contributions returned
        → Re-enrolment: every 3 years from staging date (on re-enrolment date)

Step 7: Ongoing duties
        → Monitor workforce for new eligible jobholders
        → Re-enrol every 3 years (can postpone by up to 3 months)
        → Keep records for 6 years (4 years for opt-out notices)
        → Complete re-declaration of compliance with TPR
```

### TPR Enforcement

| Penalty | Amount | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| **Fixed penalty notice** | £400 | Failure to comply with statutory notice |
| **Escalating daily penalty** | £50–£10,000/day (based on employer size) | Continued non-compliance |
| **Civil penalty** | Up to £50,000 | Wilful non-compliance |
| **Criminal prosecution** | Unlimited fine / imprisonment | Inducement to opt out; wilful failure |

Employer size bands for escalating penalties:
- 1–4 workers: £50/day
- 5–49 workers: £500/day
- 50–249 workers: £2,500/day
- 250–499 workers: £5,000/day
- 500+ workers: £10,000/day

## 4. Retained EU Law Tracker

**Primary legislation:** Retained EU Law (Revocation and Reform) Act 2023 (REUL Act 2023), in force from 1 January 2024.

### Background

Following Brexit, EU law was incorporated into UK law by the European Union
(Withdrawal) Act 2018 as "retained EU law." The REUL Act 2023 renamed this body of
law as **"assimilated law"** from 1 January 2024 and made fundamental changes.

### Key Changes from 1 January 2024

| Change | Effect |
|---|---|
| **~600 statutory instruments revoked** | Listed in Schedule 1 to the REUL Act 2023; mostly obsolete or superseded |
| **EU supremacy abolished** | Assimilated law no longer takes priority over UK domestic legislation (s.3) |
| **General principles of EU law removed** | No longer a source of rights in UK courts (s.4) |
| **CJEU case law not binding** | UK courts no longer bound by CJEU; higher courts may depart from retained CJEU case law (s.5) |
| **Terminology updated** | "Retained EU law" → "assimilated law"; "retained direct EU legislation" → "assimilated direct legislation" |

### Key Survivors — Assimilated Law Still in Force

| Instrument | Original EU Source | UK Status |
|---|---|---|
| **UK GDPR** | GDPR (EU) 2016/679 | Continues as UK GDPR alongside DPA 2018; not affected by REUL Act revocation |
| **Working Time Regulations 1998** | Working Time Directive 2003/88/EC | SI 1998/1833; 48-hour week, rest breaks, holiday entitlement (5.6 weeks) |
| **TUPE 2006** | Acquired Rights Directive 2001/23/EC | SI 2006/246; transfer of undertakings protections continue |
| **Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013** | Consumer Rights Directive 2011/83/EU | SI 2013/3134; distance selling, cooling-off rights |
| **REACH (UK)** | REACH Regulation (EC) 1907/2006 | UK REACH operated by HSE; separate registration from EU REACH |
| **CLP Regulation (UK)** | CLP Regulation (EC) 1272/2008 | Classification, labelling, packaging of chemicals |
| **Product Safety Regulations** | General Product Safety Directive 2001/95/EC | Consumer Protection Act 1987 + various sector SIs; UKCA marking |
| **Procurement Regulations** | Public Procurement Directives | Being replaced by Procurement Act 2023 (expected full force 2025) |
| **Financial Services (EU Exit)** | Various EU financial directives/regulations | Onshored by the Financial Services and Markets Act 2023 |

### Implications for Businesses

1. **Interpretation:** Courts may depart from CJEU case law — existing interpretations
   may shift over time
2. **Amendment:** Government can more easily amend assimilated law using secondary
   legislation powers under REUL Act s.12–15
3. **Compliance:** Businesses operating in both UK and EU must comply with both UK
   assimilated law and current EU law where applicable (dual compliance burden)
4. **UKCA marking:** UK product standards may diverge from EU CE marking requirements;
   transitional acceptance of CE marking extended to 2027 for most products

## 5. IR35 Status Indicator (Off-Payroll Working)

**Primary legislation:** Income Tax (Earnings and Pensions) Act 2003 (ITEPA 2003),
Chapter 10 (intermediaries). Reformed by Finance Act 2021.

### Who Determines Status?

| Sector | Who Decides? | Legislation |
|---|---|---|
| **Public sector** | End client | ITEPA 2003 s.61K–61N (from April 2017) |
| **Medium/large private sector** | End client | ITEPA 2003 s.61K–61N (from April 2021) |
| **Small private sector** | PSC / contractor | Original IR35 rules (ITEPA 2003 Chapter 8, s.48–61) |

**Small company exemption** (Companies Act 2006 s.382): 2 of 3 — turnover ≤£10.2m,
balance sheet ≤£5.1m, ≤50 employees.

### IR35 Status Assessment Workflow

Use this guided assessment to evaluate whether a contract is likely inside or outside
IR35. This is indicative only — HMRC's CEST tool is the official (though non-binding)
assessment. The definitive test comes from case law, particularly *Ready Mixed Concrete
(South East) Ltd v Minister of Pensions [1968]* and *Atholl House Productions Ltd v
HMRC [2022]*.

```
FACTOR 1: MUTUALITY OF OBLIGATION (MOO)
   → Is the end client obliged to offer work?                    YES / NO
   → Is the worker obliged to accept work offered?               YES / NO
   → IF both YES → Points INSIDE (+1)
   → IF either NO → Points OUTSIDE (+1)

FACTOR 2: CONTROL
   → Does the end client dictate WHAT work is done?              YES / NO
   → Does the end client dictate HOW the work is done?           YES / NO
   → Does the end client dictate WHEN the work is done?          YES / NO
   → Does the end client dictate WHERE the work is done?         YES / NO
   → IF significant control over how/when/where → Points INSIDE (+1 per YES)
   → IF worker has autonomy → Points OUTSIDE (+1 per NO on how/when/where)

FACTOR 3: PERSONAL SERVICE
   → Must the worker perform the services personally?            YES / NO
   → Is there a genuine, unfettered right of substitution?       YES / NO
   → Has substitution actually occurred?                         YES / NO
   → IF personal service required, no substitution → Points INSIDE (+2)
   → IF genuine right of substitution exercised → Points OUTSIDE (+2)

FACTOR 4: FINANCIAL RISK
   → Does the worker risk own capital?                           YES / NO
   → Can the worker profit from sound management?                YES / NO
   → Does the worker bear cost of rectifying defective work?     YES / NO
   → Must the worker carry professional indemnity insurance?     YES / NO
   → IF financial risk present → Points OUTSIDE (+1 per YES)
   → IF no financial risk → Points INSIDE (+1)

FACTOR 5: EQUIPMENT AND PREMISES
   → Does the worker provide own significant equipment?          YES / NO
   → Does the worker work from own premises?                     YES / NO
   → IF worker provides own → Points OUTSIDE (+1 per YES)
   → IF client provides all → Points INSIDE (+1)

FACTOR 6: PART AND PARCEL OF THE ORGANISATION
   → Does the worker have a company email / ID badge?            YES / NO
   → Does the worker attend staff meetings?                      YES / NO
   → Is the worker managed like an employee?                     YES / NO
   → Does the worker receive employee benefits?                  YES / NO
   → IF integrated → Points INSIDE (+1 per YES)
   → IF independent → Points OUTSIDE (+1 per NO)

FACTOR 7: BUSINESS ON OWN ACCOUNT
   → Does the worker have multiple clients?                      YES / NO
   → Does the worker advertise services to the market?           YES / NO
   → Does the worker have a business website / branding?         YES / NO
   → Is the worker registered for VAT?                           YES / NO
   → IF in business on own account → Points OUTSIDE (+1 per YES)
   → IF solely dependent on one client → Points INSIDE (+1)

SCORING:
   Total INSIDE points:  ___
   Total OUTSIDE points: ___

   → IF INSIDE > OUTSIDE: Contract likely CAUGHT by IR35
     → Tax and NIC due on deemed employment payment
     → End client (or agency) must deduct PAYE/NIC at source

   → IF OUTSIDE > INSIDE: Contract likely OUTSIDE IR35
     → Worker paid gross through PSC
     → Normal corporation tax and dividend planning applies

   → IF roughly equal: BORDERLINE — seek specialist advice and consider
     using HMRC's Check Employment Status for Tax (CEST) tool at
     gov.uk/guidance/check-employment-status-for-tax

   IMPORTANT: No single factor is determinative. The assessment requires a
   holistic view of the actual working arrangement, not just the contract terms.
   Keep contemporaneous evidence of how the engagement operates in practice.
```

### Consequences of IR35 Applying

- Deemed employment payment calculated at year-end (or engagement end)
- PAYE income tax and Class 1 NICs due on 95% of payments received (5% allowance for PSC costs)
- In reformed rules: liability falls on the fee-payer (agency or end client), not the PSC
- Penalties for non-compliance: inaccuracy penalties (Sch 24 FA 2007) plus interest

### Status Determination Statement (SDS)

Under the reformed off-payroll rules, the end client must:
1. Issue a Status Determination Statement to the worker and the next party in the chain
2. Take reasonable care in reaching the determination
3. Have a client-led disagreement process
4. Retain SDS for 6 years from end of relevant tax year

## 6. Regulator-Specific Deep Dives

### ICO — Data Protection Enforcement

**Key powers under DPA 2018:**

| Power | Description | Maximum Penalty |
|---|---|---|
| Information notice | Compel disclosure of information | Criminal offence to fail to comply |
| Assessment notice | Inspect data processing operations | Obstruction is criminal offence |
| Enforcement notice | Require specific action or cessation | Failure to comply: contempt of court |
| Monetary penalty notice | Financial penalty for serious breaches | £17,500,000 or 4% global annual turnover |
| Criminal prosecution | Unlawful obtaining of data (s.170 DPA 2018) | Unlimited fine |

**ICO Regulatory Action Policy** prioritises cases involving:
- Large-scale data breaches affecting many individuals
- Children's data (Age Appropriate Design Code)
- Intrusive surveillance or profiling
- Repeated failures despite guidance

### FCA — Financial Services Regulation

**Key principles for businesses** (FCA Handbook PRIN 2.1):

| Principle | Name |
|---|---|
| 1 | Integrity |
| 2 | Skill, care and diligence |
| 3 | Management and control |
| 4 | Financial prudence |
| 5 | Market conduct |
| 6 | Customers' interests |
| 7 | Communications with clients |
| 8 | Conflicts of interest |
| 9 | Customers: relationships of trust |
| 10 | Clients' assets |
| 11 | Relations with regulators |
| 12 | Consumer Duty (from July 2023) |

**Consumer Duty (Principle 12):** Cross-cutting rules requiring firms to act in good
faith, avoid foreseeable harm, and enable customers to pursue their financial
objectives. Four outcomes: products and services, price and value, consumer
understanding, consumer support.

### HSE — Workplace Health and Safety

**Employer duties under HSWA 1974 s.2:**
- Safe plant and systems of work
- Safe use, handling, storage, and transport of articles and substances
- Information, instruction, training, and supervision
- Safe place of work with safe access and egress
- Safe working environment and adequate welfare facilities

**Key regulations:**
- Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999 (risk assessments)
- Workplace (Health, Safety and Welfare) Regulations 1992
- Manual Handling Operations Regulations 1992
- Personal Protective Equipment at Work Regulations 1992
- COSHH Regulations 2002
- Reporting of Injuries, Diseases and Dangerous Occurrences Regulations 2013 (RIDDOR)
