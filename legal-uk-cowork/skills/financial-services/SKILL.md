## 1. The Regulatory Framework

### Key Legislation

| Statute | Role |
|---------|------|
| Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (FSMA) | Primary legislation establishing the UK regulatory architecture |
| Financial Services Act 2012 | Created the FCA/PRA twin-peaks model (replaced the FSA) |
| Financial Services and Markets Act 2023 (FSM Act 2023) | Post-Brexit revocation of retained EU law; cryptoasset regulation; Designated Activities Regime |
| Payment Services Regulations 2017 (SI 2017/752) | Payment services authorisation and conduct |
| Electronic Money Regulations 2011 (SI 2011/99) | E-money issuance |

### Regulatory Bodies

| Body | Statutory Objective | Scope |
|------|-------------------|-------|
| FCA (Financial Conduct Authority) | Consumer protection, market integrity, effective competition (s.1B-1E FSMA) | Conduct regulation for all authorised firms; prudential for solo-regulated firms |
| PRA (Prudential Regulation Authority) | Safety and soundness of firms; policyholder protection (s.2B-2H FSMA) | Prudential regulation of banks, insurers, major investment firms |
| Bank of England | Financial stability | Systemic oversight, resolution authority |
| FOS (Financial Ombudsman Service) | Dispute resolution | Consumer complaints against regulated firms |
| FSCS (Financial Services Compensation Scheme) | Compensation of last resort | When authorised firms fail |

## 2. Authorisation and the General Prohibition

### The General Prohibition (s.19 FSMA 2000)

No person may carry on a regulated activity in the United Kingdom unless they are:
- An authorised person (s.19(1)(a)); or
- An exempt person (s.19(1)(b)).

Breach is a criminal offence: up to 2 years' imprisonment and/or unlimited fine (s.23(1)). Agreements made in breach may be unenforceable (s.26-28).

### Regulated Activities (Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (Regulated Activities) Order 2001 — RAO, SI 2001/544)

Key regulated activities include:
- Accepting deposits (Art.5).
- Effecting/carrying out contracts of insurance (Art.10).
- Dealing in investments as principal or agent (Art.14, 21).
- Arranging deals in investments (Art.25).
- Managing investments (Art.37).
- Advising on investments (Art.53).
- Providing credit (entering into regulated credit agreements — Art.60B).
- Operating a payment system, providing payment services (PSR 2017).
- Operating a cryptoasset exchange / custody (from FSM Act 2023, when brought into force).

### Exemptions and Exclusions

| Category | Basis |
|----------|-------|
| Appointed representatives | s.39 FSMA — principal firm takes regulatory responsibility |
| Professional firms | Part XX FSMA (s.327) — regulated activity incidental to professional services (e.g., solicitors) |
| Exclusions in the RAO | Various articles excluding activities from regulation (e.g., Art.72 groups, Art.33 own-account dealing) |
| Overseas persons | Art.72 RAO — limited activities without UK authorisation |

## 4. Consumer Duty (PS22/9, Principle 12)

### Overview

The Consumer Duty came into force:
- **31 July 2023**: for new and existing products/services open to sale or renewal.
- **31 July 2024**: for closed products/services (no longer available for sale but with existing customers).

### Structure

| Layer | Requirement | Reference |
|-------|------------|-----------|
| Principle 12 | "A firm must act to deliver good outcomes for retail customers" | PRIN 2.1.1R(12) |
| Cross-cutting rules | Act in good faith; avoid foreseeable harm; enable and support customers to pursue their financial objectives | PRIN 2A.2 |
| **Four Outcomes** | | |
| 1. Products and services | Products designed to meet target market needs; distribution strategy appropriate | PROD 4 |
| 2. Price and value | Fair value — relationship between price and benefits | PRIN 2A.4 |
| 3. Consumer understanding | Communications support informed decision-making | PRIN 2A.5 |
| 4. Consumer support | Customers can use products as reasonably anticipated; no unreasonable barriers | PRIN 2A.6 |

### Practical Compliance Steps

1. **Target market identification**: define and document the target market for each product.
2. **Value assessment**: conduct a fair value assessment; identify where charges may cause foreseeable harm.
3. **Communications review**: test customer-facing communications for clarity and comprehension.
4. **Customer journey mapping**: audit customer journeys for friction and barriers (e.g., cancellation pathways must be as easy as sign-up).
5. **Governance**: board-level Consumer Duty Champion; annual board report on outcomes.
6. **Monitoring**: ongoing monitoring of outcomes data, including complaints, conversion rates, claims acceptance, and customer satisfaction.

## 5. Financial Promotion (s.21 FSMA 2000)

### The Restriction

No person may, in the course of business, communicate an invitation or inducement to engage in investment activity unless (s.21(1)):
- (a) They are an authorised person; or
- (b) The content is approved by an authorised person.

Breach is a criminal offence: up to 2 years' imprisonment and/or unlimited fine (s.25).

### Financial Promotion Gateway (from 7 February 2024)

- Authorised firms that approve financial promotions for unauthorised persons must be on the FCA's register as an "s.21 approver" (new s.21(2A) FSMA inserted by FSM Act 2023 s.60).
- Gateway application required; FCA assesses competence and expertise to approve promotions in the relevant category.
- Exemptions: certain categories of promotion (e.g., high net worth, certified sophisticated, associations of high net worth) may still be communicated without gateway approval under the Financial Promotion Order 2005 (SI 2005/1529).

### Cryptoasset Financial Promotions

- From **8 October 2023**: cryptoasset promotions brought within s.21 FSMA by the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (Financial Promotion) (Amendment) Order 2023 (SI 2023/612).
- Qualifying cryptoassets: exchange tokens, utility tokens (excluding e-money, security tokens already caught, NFTs, CBDCs).
- Requirements: 24-hour cooling-off for first-time investors, risk warnings, appropriateness assessments, ban on refer-a-friend bonuses.
- Enforcement: FCA can and does issue takedown requests and publish alerts for non-compliant promotions.

## 6. Payment Services Regulations 2017 (SI 2017/752)

### Authorised/Registered Payment Institutions

| Category | Net Worth | Requirement |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Authorised Payment Institution (API) | Higher volume / range of services | Full FCA authorisation; safeguarding; min capital |
| Registered (Small) Payment Institution (SPI) | Monthly average payment transactions <EUR 3m | Registration only; limited safeguarding |
| Electronic Money Institution (EMI) | Issues electronic money | Authorised under EMRs 2011; initial capital EUR 350k |
| Registered (Small) EMI | Outstanding e-money <EUR 5m | Registration; simplified requirements |

### Payment Services (Reg.2, Sch.1)

Services that require authorisation/registration:
1. Cash deposits and withdrawals from a payment account.
2. Execution of payment transactions (direct debits, standing orders, card payments).
3. Issuing payment instruments / acquiring payment transactions.
4. Money remittance.
5. Payment initiation services (PIS).
6. Account information services (AIS).

### Safeguarding (Reg.23-26)

- Customer funds must be safeguarded from the moment they are received.
- Method 1: segregated account at an authorised credit institution.
- Method 2: insurance policy or guarantee from an authorised insurer/credit institution.
- Funds must be identified separately from the firm's own funds at all times.

### Strong Customer Authentication (SCA) (Reg.100)

- Required for electronic payment transactions initiated by the payer.
- Two of three factors: knowledge (password/PIN), possession (phone/token), inherence (biometric).
- Exemptions: low-value transactions (<£30, cumulative <£100), trusted beneficiaries, recurring transactions of same amount to same payee.

## 7. FSM Act 2023 — Cryptoasset Provisions

### Bringing Crypto into the Regulatory Perimeter

The Financial Services and Markets Act 2023 provides powers to:
- Define "cryptoasset" and bring cryptoasset activities into the regulated activities framework (s.69, amending FSMA Sch.2).
- Create a Designated Activities Regime (DAR) (Part 4, ss.71-83) — allows regulation of activities that are not "regulated activities" under Part 4A FSMA.
- Regulate stablecoins as a form of payment (amendments to PSR 2017 and EMRs 2011).
- Extend market abuse provisions (Part 7 FSMA) to cryptoasset markets.

### Regulatory Roadmap (FCA CP/DP Series)

| Phase | Scope | Expected Timeline |
|-------|-------|-------------------|
| Phase 1 | Fiat-backed stablecoins used for payments — issuance and custody | 2024-2025 |
| Phase 2 | Cryptoasset trading platforms, intermediaries, lending/borrowing | 2025-2026 |
| Phase 3 | Market abuse regime for cryptoassets | TBD |
| Ongoing | Anti-money laundering registration (MLR 2017 Part 11A — already in force since 10 January 2020) | In force |

### Current Crypto AML Registration

- All UK cryptoasset exchange providers and custodian wallet providers must be registered with the FCA under the Money Laundering, Terrorist Financing and Transfer of Funds (Information on the Payer) Regulations 2017 (SI 2017/692) Part 11A.
- FCA has rejected a significant proportion of applications and maintains a list of unregistered cryptoasset businesses.

## 8. Complaints and the Financial Ombudsman Service

### Internal Complaints Handling (DISP 1)

- Firms must have a written complaints procedure.
- Must acknowledge within **5 business days** (DISP 1.6.2AR).
- Must issue a final response within **8 weeks** (15 business days for payment service complaints) (DISP 1.6.2R).
- If the firm cannot resolve within 8 weeks, it must issue a "summary resolution communication" or a final response letter explaining the right to refer to FOS.

### Financial Ombudsman Service (DISP 2-3)

- Consumer may refer complaint to FOS if dissatisfied with final response or after 8 weeks without response.
- Time limits: 6 months from final response letter; or 6 years from act/omission (or 3 years from when complainant knew/should have known).
- FOS decides on a "fair and reasonable" basis (s.228 FSMA; DISP 3.6.1R).

### FOS Compensation Limits (DISP 3.7.4R)

| Complaint Type | Date of Referral | Limit |
|---------------|------------------|-------|
| General complaints | On or after 1 April 2024 | £430,000 |
| General complaints (act before 1 April 2019) | On or after 1 April 2024 | £195,000 |
| Payment Protection Insurance (PPI) | N/A | £430,000 |

Awards are binding on the firm if accepted by the complainant.

## 9. Senior Managers and Certification Regime (SM&CR)

### Scope

- Banking sector: in force from 7 March 2016.
- Solo-regulated firms (insurers and FCA-only firms): in force from 9 December 2019.
- Benchmark administrators: from 7 December 2020.

### Three Tiers

| Tier | Applies To | Key Requirements |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| Core | All FCA solo-regulated firms (unless enhanced or limited scope) | Senior manager approval, Conduct Rules for all staff |
| Enhanced | Significant IFPRU firms, CASS large firms, firms with >£50bn AUM | All Core requirements + prescribed responsibilities, management responsibility maps, certification, handover procedures |
| Limited Scope | Sole traders, appointed representatives, limited permission firms | Reduced obligations; fewer prescribed senior management functions |

### Senior Management Functions (SMFs)

| SMF | Role | Source |
|-----|------|--------|
| SMF1 | Chief Executive | SUP 10C.4 |
| SMF3 | Executive Director | SUP 10C.4 |
| SMF9 | Chairman | SUP 10C.4 |
| SMF16 | Compliance Oversight | SUP 10C.4 |
| SMF17 | Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO) | SUP 10C.4 |
| SMF24 | Chief Finance Function | SUP 10C.4 (enhanced only) |
| SMF27 | Partner | SUP 10C.4 |
| SMF29 | Limited Scope (significant management) | SUP 10C.4 (limited scope firms) |

### Conduct Rules (COCON)

- **Individual Conduct Rules** (COCON 2.1): apply to almost all employees.
  1. Act with integrity.
  2. Act with due skill, care, and diligence.
  3. Be open and cooperative with regulators.
  4. Pay due regard to customer interests and treat them fairly.
  5. Observe proper standards of market conduct.

- **Senior Manager Conduct Rules** (COCON 2.2): additional rules for SMF holders.
  - SC1: Ensure effective control of area of responsibility.
  - SC2: Ensure compliance with regulatory requirements.
  - SC3: Ensure effective delegation.
  - SC4: Disclose information the FCA/PRA would reasonably expect.

### Duty of Responsibility (s.66A(5) FSMA)

A senior manager is guilty of misconduct if a contravention occurs in their area of responsibility and they did not take reasonable steps to prevent or stop it. This reverses the burden — the senior manager must show they took reasonable steps.
