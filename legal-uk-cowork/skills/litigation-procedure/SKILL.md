# England & Wales Civil Litigation Procedure

Primary legislation: **Civil Procedure Rules 1998** (SI 1998/3132), as amended.
Supplementary: **Practice Directions**, **Pre-Action Protocols**, **Court Fees Order 2008** (SI 2008/1053, as amended).

---

## 1. The Overriding Objective (CPR r.1.1)

The court must deal with cases justly and at proportionate cost. This includes:

- Ensuring parties are on an equal footing and can participate fully (r.1.1(2)(a))
- Saving expense (r.1.1(2)(b))
- Dealing with cases in ways proportionate to the amount, importance, complexity, and financial position of each party (r.1.1(2)(c))
- Ensuring cases are dealt with expeditiously and fairly (r.1.1(2)(d))
- Allotting an appropriate share of the court's resources (r.1.1(2)(e))
- Enforcing compliance with rules, practice directions, and orders (r.1.1(2)(f))

Parties must help the court further the overriding objective (r.1.3). Failure to do so
may result in adverse costs orders or strike-out.

## 2. Pre-Action Protocols

Before issuing a claim, parties must comply with the relevant pre-action protocol.
Non-compliance may result in costs sanctions (CPR r.44.3(5)(a)).

### General Pre-Action Conduct (PD — Pre-Action Conduct and Protocols)

Where no specific protocol applies:
1. Claimant sends a Letter of Claim setting out the basis of the claim
2. Defendant has a reasonable time to investigate and respond (typically 14 days acknowledgment, substantive response within a reasonable period)
3. Parties consider ADR (mediation, negotiation, early neutral evaluation)
4. Parties exchange key documents

### Specific Protocols

| Protocol | Response Time | Key Features |
|---|---|---|
| **Personal Injury** | 21 days ack / 3 months response | Low-value PI portal (RTA ≤£25k, EL/PL ≤£25k), medical report mandatory |
| **Professional Negligence** | 21 days ack / 3 months response | Detailed Letter of Claim with chronology; defendant must disclose key documents |
| **Construction & Engineering** | 14 days ack / 28 days response | Pre-action meeting required; narrow focus on disputed issues |
| **Debt Claims** | 30 days response | Information Sheet and Reply Form; signpost to free debt advice |
| **Disease & Illness** | 21 days ack / 3 months response | Letter of Claim with outline medical evidence |
| **Housing Disrepair** | 20 working days response | Expert report required before issue unless urgency |
| **Judicial Review** | 14 days response | Letter before action must give clear notice; consider Aarhus claims |

### Consequences of Non-Compliance

- Costs sanctions: indemnity costs, penalties on interest
- Proceedings stayed for compliance
- Court may refuse to grant relief to a non-compliant party

## 3. Issuing a Claim

### Claim Form (N1)

Filed at the County Court or High Court. Key steps:

1. Complete Form N1 (or N1(CC) for County Court, N208 for Part 8 claims)
2. Prepare Particulars of Claim (may be on N1 or separate — must be served within 14 days of service of claim form)
3. Pay appropriate court fee (see Section 10 below)
4. Serve claim form within 4 months of issue (6 months for service out of jurisdiction) (CPR r.7.5)

### Service Methods (CPR Part 6)

- First class post or document exchange: deemed served 2nd business day after posting
- Personal service: date of delivery
- Email (where consented): deemed served next business day if sent after 4:30pm
- Fax: deemed served next business day if sent after 4:30pm

### Response to Claim

Defendant must respond within 14 days of service (or 28 days if acknowledgment of service filed):
- **Admit** (Form N9A/N9B): full or partial admission
- **Defend** (Form N9B/N9D): file defence within 14/28 days
- **Counterclaim**: filed with defence
- **Default judgment** (CPR Part 12): if no response, claimant may enter judgment

## 4. Track Allocation (CPR Part 26)

After defences are filed, the court sends an Allocation Questionnaire (Form N150).

| Track | Claim Value | Trial Length | Key Features |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Small Claims** | ≤£10,000 (PI ≤£1,000) | Up to 1 day | Limited costs recovery; informal hearing; no disclosure beyond documents relied on; no expert evidence without permission |
| **Fast Track** | £10,001–£25,000 | ≤1 day | Standard disclosure; 1 expert per party per field; fixed trial costs; strict timetable (typically 30 weeks to trial) |
| **Intermediate Track** | £25,001–£100,000 | 1–3 days | Introduced 1 Oct 2023; fixed recoverable costs; assigned complexity band (1–4); limited disclosure |
| **Multi-Track** | £25,001+ (or complex) | No limit | Costs budgeting (cases <£10m); case management conferences; extensive disclosure possible |

The court has discretion to allocate to a higher or lower track considering:
- Financial value of the claim
- Remedies sought
- Complexity of facts, law, evidence, and number of parties
- Importance of the claim to non-parties
- Oral evidence required
- Views of the parties

## 5. Part 36 Offers (CPR Part 36)

A formal settlement mechanism with significant costs consequences.

### Making an Offer

- Must be in writing, state it is made under Part 36
- Specify a relevant period (minimum 21 days)
- State whether it relates to the whole or part of the claim
- State whether it takes into account any counterclaim

### Cost Consequences — Claimant Fails to Beat Defendant's Part 36 Offer

Where the claimant fails to obtain a judgment more advantageous than the defendant's
Part 36 offer (CPR r.36.17(3)):

- Claimant pays defendant's costs from the date of expiry of the relevant period
- Assessed on the **standard basis** unless the court orders otherwise
- Interest on those costs

### Cost Consequences — Claimant Beats Own Part 36 Offer

Where judgment is at least as advantageous to the claimant as their own Part 36 offer
(CPR r.36.17(4)):

- **Interest on damages** at up to 10% above base rate from the date of expiry
- **Costs on the indemnity basis** from the date of expiry
- **Additional amount** of up to 10% of the first £500,000 of damages awarded, plus 5% of any amount above £500,000 (capped at £75,000)
- Interest on costs at up to 10% above base rate

The court may disapply these consequences if it considers it unjust (r.36.17(5)).

## 6. Costs

### Basis of Assessment

| Basis | Standard (r.44.3(2)) | Indemnity (r.44.3(3)) |
|---|---|---|
| **Test** | Proportionate to matters in issue | Need not be proportionate |
| **Doubt** | Resolved in favour of the paying party | Resolved in favour of the receiving party |
| **When ordered** | Default basis | Unreasonable conduct; Part 36 consequences |

### Costs Budgeting (CPR Part 3, Section II)

Applies to all multi-track cases where the claim is valued at less than £10m
(excluding personal injury claims valued at £10m or above).

- Parties must file Precedent H costs budget
- Budget phases: pre-action, issue/statements of case, CMC, disclosure, witness statements, expert reports, PTR, trial preparation, trial, ADR
- Court will make a costs management order recording approved or agreed budgets
- At assessment, the court will not depart from the approved budget unless there is good reason (r.3.18)

### Fixed Recoverable Costs (CPR Part 45 — from 1 October 2023)

Applies to fast track and intermediate track claims.

**Fast Track (≤£25,000):**
- Fixed costs grid based on stage at which claim settles or proceeds to trial
- Limited additional amounts for specific work (e.g., injunction applications)

**Intermediate Track (£25,001–£100,000):**
- Four complexity bands determining costs limits
- Band 1: simpler claims (e.g., road traffic, employer's liability)
- Band 2: moderate complexity
- Band 3: higher complexity (e.g., clinical negligence, professional negligence)
- Band 4: most complex permitted in the track
- Court may assign a claim to a higher band in exceptional circumstances

### Qualified One-Way Costs Shifting (QOCS) — CPR r.44.13–44.17

Applies to personal injury claims. The claimant is generally protected from paying the
defendant's costs if the claim fails, unless:
- Claim is struck out as disclosing no reasonable grounds or being an abuse
- Claim is found to be fundamentally dishonest (s.57 Criminal Justice and Courts Act 2015)

## 7. Disclosure (CPR Part 31 & PD 57AD)

### Standard Disclosure (CPR r.31.6)

Applies in most multi-track cases outside the Business & Property Courts:
- Documents on which a party relies
- Documents that adversely affect own case or another party's case
- Documents that support another party's case
- Documents required by a relevant practice direction

### Extended Disclosure — PD 57AD (Business & Property Courts)

Replaces standard disclosure in the Business & Property Courts. Parties must:

1. Complete Disclosure Review Document (before first CMC)
2. Propose disclosure models for each Issue for Disclosure

| Model | Description |
|---|---|
| **Model A** | No order for disclosure (known documents + relied-upon documents only) |
| **Model B** | Limited disclosure — request-based, specific documents or narrow classes |
| **Model C** | Request-based — particular documents or classes by reasonable and proportionate search |
| **Model D** | Narrow search-based — disclosure of documents identified by agreed search terms |
| **Model E** | Wide search-based — broad search, akin to former standard disclosure; ordered only where proportionate |

### Key Obligations

- Duty of disclosure is continuing (r.31.11)
- Documents must be listed on Form N265 (disclosure list)
- Privilege: legal professional privilege (legal advice + litigation), without prejudice, common interest
- Failure to disclose: adverse inferences, strike-out, costs sanctions

## 8. Money Claims Online (MCOL)

**URL:** moneyclaim.gov.uk

- Online claims for debts up to £100,000
- Lower court fees than paper filing
- Available 24/7 (subject to maintenance)
- Claimant must have a UK address
- Cannot be used for personal injury, Housing Act claims, or claims against the Crown

### Process

1. Register at moneyclaim.gov.uk
2. Enter defendant details and claim amount
3. Pay court fee online (slightly reduced fees apply)
4. Claim issued electronically; served by HMCTS by first class post
5. Defendant responds online or by post within 14 days (28 if acknowledgment filed)
6. If no response, request default judgment online
7. If defended, case transfers to appropriate County Court

## 11. Enforcement of Judgments

Where a judgment debtor fails to pay, the judgment creditor can apply for enforcement:

| Method | Form | Description | Fee |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Warrant of control** (County Court) | N323 | Bailiff seizes goods | £77 |
| **Writ of control** (High Court, >£600) | N293A / PF86A | HCEO seizes goods | £66 + HCEO fees |
| **Attachment of earnings** | N337 | Deductions from debtor's wages | £110 |
| **Third party debt order** | N349 | Freeze and seize bank accounts | £110 |
| **Charging order** | N379 | Charge on debtor's property | £110 |
| **Order for sale** | N/A (Part 73) | Sale of charged property | £275 |
| **Order to obtain information** | N316 | Debtor attends court to disclose assets | £55 |

Transfer up to the High Court for enforcement is available for judgments exceeding
£600 (s.42 County Courts Act 1984). High Court Enforcement Officers often achieve
higher recovery rates than County Court bailiffs.

## 12. Limitation Periods

Key limitation periods (Limitation Act 1980):

| Claim Type | Period | Statute |
|---|---|---|
| Contract (simple) | 6 years | s.5 |
| Contract (deed) | 12 years | s.8 |
| Tort (general) | 6 years | s.2 |
| Personal injury | 3 years (with s.33 discretion) | s.11 |
| Defamation | 1 year | s.4A |
| Contribution claims | 2 years from judgment/settlement | s.10 |
| Land recovery | 12 years | s.15 |
| Judgment enforcement | 6 years | s.24 |

Date of knowledge provisions (s.14, s.14A) may extend the limitation period,
particularly in latent damage and professional negligence cases.

## 13. Practical Workflow: Bringing a Debt Claim

```
Step 1: Send Letter Before Claim (Pre-Action Protocol for Debt Claims)
        → Allow 30 days for response
        → Include Information Sheet and Reply Form

Step 2: If no response or no settlement → Issue claim
        → Complete Form N1 (or use MCOL for claims ≤£100k)
        → Pay court fee per table above
        → Serve on defendant

Step 3: Await response (14 days, or 28 with acknowledgment)
        → If no response → Apply for default judgment (Part 12)
        → If admitted → Request judgment on admission (Part 14)
        → If defended → Await allocation

Step 4: Allocation and directions
        → Complete Form N150 (Directions Questionnaire)
        → Court allocates to track
        → Follow directions timetable

Step 5: Trial (or settlement)
        → Consider Part 36 offer to protect costs position
        → Attend trial; judgment entered

Step 6: Enforcement (if judgment debtor does not pay)
        → Apply for Order to Obtain Information (N316)
        → Select appropriate enforcement method
        → Consider instructing HCEO for debts >£600
```
