## 1. Legal Estates and Interests (Law of Property Act 1925)

### The Two Legal Estates (s.1(1) LPA 1925)

Only two estates in land can exist at law:
1. **Fee simple absolute in possession** (freehold) — s.1(1)(a).
2. **Term of years absolute** (leasehold) — s.1(1)(b).

All other estates take effect in equity only (s.1(3)).

### Legal Interests (s.1(2) LPA 1925)

The following can exist as legal interests:
- Easements, rights, and privileges equivalent to an estate in fee simple or term of years absolute (s.1(2)(a)).
- Rentcharges in possession (s.1(2)(b)).
- Charges by way of legal mortgage (s.1(2)(c)).
- Any other similar charges not created by instrument (s.1(2)(d)).
- Rights of entry (s.1(2)(e)).

### Formalities

| Transaction | Requirement | Section |
|-------------|------------|---------|
| Contracts for sale of land | In writing, signed by both parties, containing all expressly agreed terms | s.2 Law of Property (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 1989 |
| Transfer/creation of legal estate | By deed (signed, witnessed, delivered) | s.52(1) LPA 1925; s.1 LP(MP)A 1989 |
| Short leases (3 years or less) | No deed required; must take effect in possession at best rent with no fine | s.54(2) LPA 1925 |
| Equitable interests | In writing signed by person creating the interest | s.53(1)(a) LPA 1925 |
| Declaration of trust of land | In writing signed by declarer | s.53(1)(b) LPA 1925 |

## 2. Land Registration (Land Registration Act 2002)

### Compulsory Registration Triggers (s.4 LRA 2002)

Registration at HM Land Registry is compulsory on:
- Transfer of freehold (s.4(1)(a)).
- Grant of lease for more than 7 years (s.4(1)(c)).
- Transfer of lease with more than 7 years to run (s.4(1)(a)).
- First legal mortgage of unregistered freehold or lease with more than 7 years to run (s.4(1)(g)).
- Assent by personal representatives (s.4(1)(a)).

**Time limit**: applicant must apply within 2 months of the trigger event (s.6(4)). Failure renders the transfer void at law — the legal estate reverts to the transferor, who holds on bare trust (s.7(2)).

### Registered Interests

| Category | Effect | Examples |
|----------|--------|----------|
| Registered charges (s.23) | Legal mortgage noted on register | Bank mortgage |
| Notices (ss.32-39) | Protect third-party interests on register | Restrictive covenants, estate contracts, home rights |
| Restrictions (ss.40-47) | Limit registered proprietor's dealing powers | Trust restrictions, consent restrictions |
| Overriding interests (Sch.1 & Sch.3) | Bind purchaser even though not on register | Short leases (7 years or less), interests of persons in actual occupation, legal easements |

### Adverse Possession (Sch.6 LRA 2002)

- After 10 years, squatter may apply to be registered as proprietor.
- Land Registry notifies registered proprietor who has 65 business days to object.
- If proprietor objects, application fails unless one of three conditions met (Sch.6 para 5): estoppel, other right to land, or boundary dispute.
- If proprietor takes no action, squatter is registered.
- If proprietor objects but takes no steps to evict within 2 years, squatter may re-apply and will succeed.

## 3. Business Tenancies (Landlord and Tenant Act 1954 Part II)

### Security of Tenure (s.24)

A business tenancy does not end at the contractual expiry date — it continues automatically until terminated by one of the statutory mechanisms (s.24(1)). This applies to any tenancy where the property is occupied for the purposes of a business carried on by the tenant (s.23(1)).

### Termination Methods

| Method | Section | Notice Period | Key Requirements |
|--------|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| Landlord's s.25 notice | s.25 | 6-12 months before specified termination date | Must state whether landlord opposes renewal and on which grounds |
| Tenant's s.26 request | s.26 | 6-12 months before proposed commencement of new tenancy | Tenant proposes terms for new tenancy |
| Tenant's s.27 notice | s.27 | 3 months written notice | Tenant gives up security of tenure |
| Forfeiture | s.24(2) | Per lease terms | Breach of covenant; s.146 LPA 1925 notice required (except non-payment of rent) |

### Grounds for Opposing Renewal (s.30(1))

| Ground | Description | Mandatory? | Compensation? |
|--------|-------------|-----------|---------------|
| (a) | Tenant's failure to repair | Discretionary | No |
| (b) | Persistent delay in paying rent | Discretionary | No |
| (c) | Other substantial breaches / undesirable reasons | Discretionary | No |
| (d) | Suitable alternative accommodation available | Mandatory | No |
| (e) | Sub-letting of part where higher rent for whole | Mandatory | No |
| (f) | Landlord intends to demolish/reconstruct | Mandatory | Yes |
| (g) | Landlord intends to occupy for own business/residence | Mandatory | Yes |

Compensation for grounds (e), (f), and (g): 1x rateable value (or 2x if business occupied for 14+ years) — s.37.

### Contracting Out (s.38A)

Parties may agree to exclude security of tenure, provided:
- Landlord serves a "health warning" notice on tenant at least 14 days before the lease (or tenant makes a statutory declaration if less than 14 days).
- Tenant signs a declaration acknowledging the consequences.
- The lease itself references the agreement.
- Procedure set out in The Regulatory Reform (Business Tenancies) (England and Wales) Order 2003 (SI 2003/3096) Schedule 1-4.

## 4. Ground Rent — Leasehold Reform (Ground Rent) Act 2022

- In force from **30 June 2022** (SI 2022/694).
- Applies to new long residential leases (21+ years) granted on or after 30 June 2022.
- Ground rent is capped at a **peppercorn** (effectively zero) for qualifying leases.
- Exceptions: community housing leases, home finance plan leases, certain retirement homes (transition until 1 April 2023).
- Enforcement: Trading Standards; financial penalty of £500-£30,000 per breach (s.8).
- Does NOT apply retrospectively to existing leases — existing leaseholders retain their current ground rent obligations (reform of existing ground rents is subject to separate future legislation).

## 5. Renters' Rights Act 2025

**Royal Assent**: 27 October 2025. **Commencement**: phased, with key provisions from **1 May 2026** (SI 2026/XXX — anticipated).

### Key Provisions

| Reform | Detail | Section |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Abolition of s.21 "no-fault" evictions | All s.21 Housing Act 1988 notices abolished; landlord must use s.8 grounds | s.1 RRA 2025 |
| Periodic tenancies | All assured tenancies become periodic (no fixed term ASTs); tenancies roll monthly/fortnightly as applicable | s.2 |
| Tenant notice to quit | Tenant must give **2 months' notice** to end a periodic tenancy | s.3 |
| Rent increases | Landlord may increase rent once per year via **s.13 Housing Act 1988** mechanism only; tenant may refer to First-tier Tribunal | s.7 |
| Expanded s.8 grounds | New/amended mandatory grounds including repeated serious rent arrears (Ground 8A), sale of property after 12 months (Ground 1A), landlord moving in (Ground 1), student accommodation (Ground 4A) | s.4-6 |
| Right to keep pets | Tenant may request to keep a pet; landlord must not unreasonably refuse; landlord may require pet damage insurance | s.12 |
| Rental bidding banned | Landlords and agents must not invite or accept bids above the advertised rent | s.14 |
| Decent Homes Standard | Private rented sector must meet the Decent Homes Standard (to be defined by regulations) | s.17 |
| Landlord Ombudsman | All private landlords must join a government-approved ombudsman scheme | s.19 |
| Civil penalties | Local authority enforcement: up to **£7,000** for minor breaches, up to **£40,000** for serious or repeat breaches | s.22 |
| Rent Repayment Orders | Extended to cover more offences | s.24 |

### Transitional Provisions

- Existing fixed-term ASTs: will convert to periodic tenancies at the end of their fixed term (or on a specified date if the fixed term has already expired).
- Existing s.21 notices served before commencement: valid if served and expire before a backstop date to be set by regulations.
- Landlord notice periods for s.8 grounds vary: 2 weeks (rent arrears), 2 months (most others), 4 months (sale/landlord occupation for first 12 months of tenancy).

## 6. SDLT Calculator — Current Rates (England & Northern Ireland)

### Standard Residential Rates (from 1 April 2025)

| Band | Rate |
|------|------|
| Up to £125,000 | 0% |
| £125,001 - £250,000 | 2% |
| £250,001 - £925,000 | 5% |
| £925,001 - £1,500,000 | 10% |
| Over £1,500,000 | 12% |

### Additional Dwelling Surcharge

- **5% surcharge** on each band (from 31 October 2024, increased from previous 3%) for purchases of additional residential properties (e.g., buy-to-let, second homes) where price exceeds £40,000 (FA 2003 s.55 + Sch.4ZA as amended by FA 2025 s.X).

### First-Time Buyer Relief (FA 2003 Sch.6ZA)

| Band | Rate |
|------|------|
| Up to £300,000 | 0% |
| £300,001 - £500,000 | 5% |

- Purchase price must not exceed £500,000; otherwise standard rates apply.
- Available for purchases of a single dwelling used as main residence.

### Non-Residential / Mixed-Use Rates

| Band | Rate |
|------|------|
| Up to £150,000 | 0% |
| £150,001 - £250,000 | 2% |
| Over £250,000 | 5% |

### SDLT Worked Example (Standard Residential, £450,000)

| Slice | Rate | Tax |
|-------|------|-----|
| First £125,000 | 0% | £0 |
| Next £125,000 (£125,001-£250,000) | 2% | £2,500 |
| Remaining £200,000 (£250,001-£450,000) | 5% | £10,000 |
| **Total SDLT** | | **£12,500** |

### Filing and Payment

- SDLT return must be filed with HMRC within **14 days** of completion (FA 2003 s.76(1) as amended).
- Payment due at the same time.
- Penalties: immediate £100 penalty for late filing, rising to £200 after 3 months, then daily penalties and tax-geared penalties.

**Note**: Scotland has Land and Buildings Transaction Tax (LBTT). Wales has Land Transaction Tax (LTT). This calculator covers England and Northern Ireland only.

## 8. Practical Considerations for SMBs

### Taking a Business Lease — Checklist

1. Check if the lease has security of tenure under LTA 1954 Part II or if it has been contracted out (s.38A).
2. Review repairing obligations — full repairing and insuring (FRI) lease vs. internal only.
3. Check the rent review mechanism (open market, RPI-linked, fixed uplifts).
4. Service charge — review the cap, the landlord's obligation to consult (s.20 Landlord and Tenant Act 1985 for long residential leases; commercial service charges are unregulated but review reasonableness).
5. Alienation — assignment and subletting restrictions.
6. Break clauses — conditions precedent (vacant possession, no outstanding breaches, payment of rent).
7. Permitted use — check against planning use class (Use Classes Order 1987 as amended; Class E commercial, business, and service from 1 September 2020).
8. Guarantees — personal guarantees, authorised guarantee agreements (AGAs) on assignment (s.16 Landlord and Tenant (Covenants) Act 1995).

### Buying Commercial Property — Key Steps

1. Instruct solicitor and surveyor.
2. Searches: local authority (LLC1/CON29), environmental, drainage, chancel repair liability.
3. Pre-completion search OS1 (30 business day priority window).
4. Exchange contracts (deposit typically 10%).
5. Completion — transfer deed (TR1), SDLT payment, AP1 application to Land Registry.
6. Post-completion: register at Land Registry within priority period; file SDLT return within 14 days.
